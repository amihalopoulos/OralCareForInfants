Common Oral Health Conditions During Pregnancy

  1. Hormone changes when pregnant can increase signs of some dental problems such as Gum Disease (red, puffy, bleeding gums) as well as Premature Birth Weight (your baby being born too small)
  2. Gingivitis = inflammation (red, puffy bleeding) of gum tissue in the mouth
    • during pregnancy if you already have gingivitis, this gingivitis may cause a lump/tumor in the mouth
      • these are NOT cancer, but must be treated by a dentist
  3. Increased Risk of Infection
    • The immune system(which protects you from getting sick) is weakened when pregnant
    • Be careful of open cuts/sores/wounds in mouth area since they can become easily infected
  4. Dry Mouth (Cotton Mouth)
    • Treat by having good oral hygiene
      • Rinse with a non-alcoholic mouth wash and use toothpaste with fluoride
      • You can also try chewing gum
  5. Nauseous and Vomiting (Upset Stomach, Throwing Up)
    • 70-85% of pregnant women experience these symptoms
    • It is important to take ant-acids, such as TUMS/ROLAIDS/1 tsp Baking Powder + Water in order to control acid production in the stomach
    • If not controlled, enamel loss can become a problem because stomach acid erodes (wears down) teeth
  6. Dental Caries
    • Can cause cavities
    • Keep up with good oral hygiene by flossing, brushing teeth, and using mouth wash when pregnant
  7. Gestational Diabetes
    • A Condition in which women without previous history of diabetes have high blood sugar levels during pregnancy
    • Signs/symptoms = easily tired, increased thirst/drinking liquids, increased/more frequent urination (peeing)
    • Can be tried by your doctor, make sure you go to your doctor in order for them to monitor your blood sugar levels
  8. Preeclampsia (High blood pressure during pregnancy)
    • Can be a dangerous pregnancy complication characterized by high blood pressure
    • Signs/symptoms = easily tired, nausea, blurry vision, bad headaches, swelling in legs or arms, decreased amount of urine (pee), weight gain
    • Can be treated by your doctor, make sure you go to your doctor in order for them to monitor your blood pressure levels
