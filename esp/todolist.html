An Educational Tool to Teach Pregnant and New Moms about Proper Dental Care for Their Newborn or Toddler

Oral Health Check List for Pregnant Women

  • Get a dental checkup and tell your dentist that you are pregnant.
    • It is safe to have dental care when you are pregnant.
    • Talk to you medical doctor if you need help getting a dental appointment
    • Do not put it off until after you have the baby.
  • Brush your teeth twice a day (2X) with toothpaste that has fluoride.
  • Floss once a day (1X) to keep teeth and gums healthy.
  • Rinse at Night with an alcohol free mouthwash (Ask your dentist for a suggestion if you are not sure which one to get).
  • If you have ‘morning sickness’ or are throwing up/ sick to your stomach, try mixing a teaspoon of baking soda mixed in water to stop stomach acid from attacking your teeth.
  • Eat healthy foods such as vegetables, fruit and cheese.
  • Drink water and low-fat milk instead of soda.
  • Avoid foods and drinks that have a lot of sugar since they can cause tooth decay and cavities
  • If you have good oral health habits, your child will too!
    • Good oral health may reduce the risk of having a premature birth
